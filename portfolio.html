SANCTUARY was a map I created for the Mapeadores Zombie Apocalypse Mapping Contest, winning the runner up prize for best aesthetics.
Where the other maps submitted had more interesting gameplay, I aimed to create the best map visually, putting a lot of effort into the aesthetic style of the map. It's a short map, doesn't overstay its welcome, and generally I am very proud of how it turned out.

An entry and the 1st place winner of the Mapeadores Prey Inauguration Contest.
A lot of time was spent on the visuals of this map, trying new level-design techniques I had not previously used. Alongside the visuals, the gameplay was also a big factor. A lot of time was spent developing the map so it played well in a multiplayer scenario.
This map played very well during testing sessions with other players, I added a scaling mechanic so certain areas would open up with certain player-counts, which really helped to balance the map.

Collaborations

I made a few areas for MAPEADORES MUSEUM, as did many other mappers. A total of 16 mappers helped to create this map, each one making a self-contained map within 2048^3 units.

I made 3 areas, SSS-lite (a mini version of a previous popular map), DYSTOPIA (a downtown cyberpunk-ish area) and the GOLEVATOR (an elevator powered by player-chat spam).

My first map collaboration. 5 mappers made their own unique minigame that would be randomly selected to be played, followed by a "finale" after completion of all 5 minigames.

I created a simple minigame where players would spread out and find objects to destroy, while avoiding enemies. I also helped with making a few useable items and some other miscellaneous things.
I learned a lot while working on this map, and understood what it was like to work in a team on a single project.
