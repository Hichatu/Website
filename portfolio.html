An entry and the 1st place winner of the Mapeadores Prey Inauguration Contest. This was the first map I actually fully delved into making look aesthetically pleasing. A big portion of the initial development was spent on the visuals of this map. I wanted to "wow" the judges and playtesters with the distinct visual style of the map. And that seemed to land it 1st place.
Gameplay was also a big factor, trying to account for the various classes and gameplay differences from regular Zombie Mod. A lot was learned during the making of this map, in terms of both detailing and gameplay perspectives.

This was an extremely small map I made, trying to fit the final map file in under 1mb. The final size was 860kb, with still semi-decent gameplay. It was made for a mini Halloween mapping event in 2021, so the theme is this decrepid looping hallway.
Where the size is small, and the gameplay could be improved, it is still a good map for lower player-counts or some quick fun.

❮ ❯

Solgryn was my attempt at a "big map", visuals are not the best but it makes up for it in the gameplay. Entered to the 2020 Zombie Escape Mapping Contest, I did not take home any prize money.
Although I did not place within the top 5 winners, I learned a vast amount about Source Engine mapping, in terms of gameplay and other areas. I strived to be too much like existing maps with Solgryn, and didn't really offer my own unique ideas.

My first map I publically released. LOLXD is a map that still stands to this day, despite it's age and my lack of knowledge when making it.
It has a unique visual style and it's gameplay is well suited for a variety of players, offering some unique takes and a "self-aware" mindset within the map.

Collaborations

I made a few areas for MAPEADORES MUSEUM, as did many other mappers. A total of 16 mappers helped to create this map, each one making a self-contained map within 2048^3 units.
I made 3 areas, SSS-lite (a mini version of a previous popular map), DYSTOPIA (a downtown cyberpunk-ish area) and the GOLEVATOR (an elevator powered by player-chat spam).

My first map collaboration. 5 mappers made their own unique minigame that would be randomly selected to be played, followed by a "finale" after completion of all 5 minigames.
I created a simple minigame where players would spread out and find objects to destroy, while avoiding enemies, hence the name "Weeaboo Anihilation". Also helped with making a few useable items and some other miscellaneous things.
